Built for STEM & Homeschool

Real robotics and AI in your backyard — not a screen-only sim. terrAIn gives students something to build, program, and watch move real earth. One 4×8 ft bed becomes a robotics lab, a physics sandbox, and a garden.

What students learn:

  • Robotics & mechatronics — motors, sensors, ESP32, wireless control
  • Coding & systems — ROS2, micro-ROS, coordination logic, optional simulation (Isaac Sim)
  • Physics & terrain — force, friction, soil mechanics, why the world pushes back
  • Planning & failure — iterate in the real world: dig, grade, haul; debug when it doesn’t work

Fits homeschool (flexible schedule, multi-age, one kit can serve the whole family), STEM programs (clubs, co-ops, project-based units), and makers who want serious hardware without a lab. Curriculum-friendly and open source.

Why This Matters

Most AI today is disembodied. Language models reason in text but have no physical understanding of the world. Yet intelligence evolved through interaction with matter — digging, moving, building, shaping terrain.

For STEM and homeschool, that’s the hook: AI and robotics that touch the real world. Kids see cause and effect — code and soil, planning and terrain. terrAIn asks: What happens when we learn by shaping the physical world? In a raised bed or a test arena, robots move earth and students learn how the world pushes back.

System at a Glance

  • Physical terrain — 4×8 ft bed (greenhouse- and garden-bed scale), soil/sand/gravel, optional irrigation
  • Autonomous robots — Huina excavators (1550/1580), dump truck, bulldozer, loader; ESP32-S3, micro-ROS
  • Perception — overhead gantry, RGB/depth, terrain mapping
  • AI planning — ROS2 server (Jetson), coordination, deployment
  • Simulation — NVIDIA Isaac Sim for training and sim-to-real transfer

Concept: terrain bed as robotics sandbox — same footprint as a garden bed.

Controllers: Atlas DS / Tricorder

terrAIn uses Atlas DS and Tricorder as the controller platform — the dual-screen layout is ideal: bottom screen = bed view (overhead terrain map), top screen = vehicle view (camera feed from the robot).

One device gives you the big picture and the driver’s seat: plan on the bed map below, drive or monitor from the vehicle camera above. Ideal for teaching teleop and autonomy — students see both the overhead strategy view and the robot’s perspective. Manual override, teleop, and field configuration in one handheld. Same stack across the terrAIn product line.
